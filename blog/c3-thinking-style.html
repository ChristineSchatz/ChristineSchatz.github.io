My Thinking-Learning Style

Learning How I Learn

April 26, 2015


My Gregorc Thinking Style test results turned out as follows:


  1. Concrete Random

This is absolutely and completely true of my learning style:


** They learn best when they are able to use trial-and-error approaches and given the opportunity to work through the problems themselves. They like experimenting to find answers and solving problems independently.**


So far, my biggest struggles relate to pairing: keeping focused and not knowing whether or not I'm doing a good job of driving or navigating. Sometimes while pairing, my thoughts trail off a little, or I might focus on the character of my pair's voice, or what's going on in their background, or something abstract and weird, and I have to consciously check in to keep focused on the solution. I think pairing is naturally going to be a struggle for a lot of students. We've never had experience with it before, and it requires a degree of relinquishing control. And relinquishing control is probably hard for Conrete Random & Sequential thinkers.


That being said, I think I need to work on is being a little more vocal about how I learn best to my pair. Instead of struggling with a solution, and saying "Umm...uhhh, hmm...lemme think," I could say, "Hey ____, do you mind if I experiment a little and try a couple of my ideas out? I just want to see if it would work."


I also need to work on analyzing problems before trying to solve them. It's not intuitive to pseudocode, but I really need to try and pseudocode out harder challenges every single time. My instinct is often to dive right into a problem, which is not a good practice.


Thanks for reading!
