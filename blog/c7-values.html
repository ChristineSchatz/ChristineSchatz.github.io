Keeping it Positive

Combatting stereotype threat

May 24, 2015


Last week, we looked into Claude Steele's "stereotype threat" theory. Today I'll talk about what values have helped me combat stereotype threat:



Meaningful work

Close relationships

Creativity / Music

Inner harmony / tranquility

Integrity / Commitment

Recognition

Physical challenge

Nature


I've held these values for as long as I can remember. They correlate to certain situations, such as when I'm feeling happiest: feeling like my contributions at work matter, being with family and friends, creating art in Photoshop, meditating, and being in nature: hiking, running etc.


How do these values help me combat stereotype threat exactly?

They remind me that I'm human, that I make mistakes, and that I am able to overcome obstacles by cultivating these values. These values help inspire me to stay committed to my goals in life. In this sense, I can tell myself, "Nothing is going to stop me!" (very cliche, I know). By reminding myself of these happiness goals, the easier it is to get through those tough spots.


That's all for now. Thanks for reading!
